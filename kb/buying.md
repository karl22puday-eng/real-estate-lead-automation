# Crestview Realty — The Home Buying Process

Buying a home with Crestview is designed to be clear and low-stress. Here's how it works
and what to expect at each step.

## Step 1 — Get pre-approved
Before serious shopping, talk to a lender and get a **mortgage pre-approval** (not just a
pre-qualification). Pre-approval tells you your real budget and makes your offers far
stronger — in a competitive market, sellers often won't consider an offer without one.
Cash buyers skip this but should be ready to show proof of funds. We can refer trusted
local lenders if you don't have one.

## Step 2 — Define your search
We sit down (in person or over a call) to nail down: target neighborhoods, must-haves vs.
nice-to-haves, property type (house, condo, townhouse), price range, and timeline. The
tighter this is, the faster we find the right home.

## Step 3 — Tour homes
We set up showings that fit your schedule and send you new listings as they hit the
market. In fast markets, being able to tour quickly is the difference between getting the
home and missing it.

## Step 4 — Make an offer
When you find the one, we build an offer strategy: price, contingencies (financing,
inspection, appraisal), earnest money, and timing. We advise based on comparable sales
and how competitive the listing is, then negotiate on your behalf.

## Step 5 — Under contract → inspection & appraisal
Once your offer is accepted, you're "under contract." You'll do a **home inspection**
(and can renegotiate or walk if there are serious issues), and your lender orders an
**appraisal** to confirm the home's value.

## Step 6 — Closing
Final loan approval, a walkthrough, signing, and you get the keys. Typical timeline from
accepted offer to closing is about **30–45 days** with financing, faster for cash.

## Costs to plan for (buyers)
- **Down payment:** as low as 3–5% on many conventional loans; 20% avoids private
  mortgage insurance (PMI). FHA and VA options exist for those who qualify.
- **Closing costs:** roughly **2–5% of the purchase price** (lender fees, title,
  escrow, taxes).
- **Earnest money:** a good-faith deposit (often 1–3%) that applies to your purchase.
- **Buyer's agent commission:** in most transactions this is paid through the deal at
  closing, not out of the buyer's pocket — we'll always confirm this for your specific
  deal up front.

## How fast can I buy?
If you're pre-approved and decisive, you can be under contract within days and closed in
about a month. The most common delays are waiting on pre-approval and indecision on the
search — getting those handled early is the single biggest time-saver.
